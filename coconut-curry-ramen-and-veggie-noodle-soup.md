> Ramen soup with veggie heavy composition. Some flexibility in the contents, but good cool weather meal overall.
## Ingredients

1. 2 red bell peppers
2. 1 tablespoon neutral oil
3. 8 oz sliced mushrooms (any kind)
4. Kosher salt
5. 2 to 4 tablespoons red curry paste
6. 5 cups low-sodium broth (veggie or chicken)
7. 3 (3 oz) packages of instance chicken or veggie ramen noodles
8. 1 (14 to 16 oz) block of extra firm tofu
9. 3 cups fresh spinach
10. 1 lime
11. 2 tablespoon fish sauce (optional)
12. 1 tablespoon low-sodium soy sauce
---
## Preparation

1. Thinly slice the peppers
2. Warm the oil over a pot over medium-high heat. When it shimmers, add the peppers, mushrooms, and 1/2 teaspoon salt.  Cook string occasionally (using tongs) until veggies are slightly softened (3-4 minutes).
3. Stir in at least 2 Tablespoons of red curry paste, and up to 4 if you prefer more spice.  Cook stirring frequently until it darkens in color and starts to stick to the pot, another minute or two.
4. Stir in the stock, coconut milk, and 1 seasoning packet from the ramen noodle packages.  Discard the rest of the packets.  Increase the heat to high and bring to boil.
5. Meanwhile, drain the tofu and use your hands to squeeze the moisture out of it.
6. When the soup is boiling, break the tofu into bite-size pieces and add to the pot, along the ramen noodles and spinach. Cook until the noodles are tender, smooshing everything into the liquid, about 3 minutes. 
7. Cut the lime in half and squeeze in the juice. Stir in the fish sauce and soy sauce.
8. Remove from heat, divide among bowls and enjoy.

## Ingredients

FOR THE CHICKPEAS

- 2 (15-ounce) cans chickpeas, drained
- ¾ to 1 cup extra-virgin olive oil
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 teaspoons ground cumin, or 1 teaspoon each ground cumin and ground coriander
- 1 teaspoon sweet paprika
- Salt and pepper

FOR THE TAHINI SAUCE:

- ⅓ cup tahini
- Freshly squeezed juice of 1 lemon
- 1 garlic clove, minced
- Warm water, as needed

TO FINISH:

- Olive oil, for cooking
- About 10 ounces kale, chard or spinach, well washed and thick stems removed
- 3 cups pita chips
- Handful of roughly chopped flat-leaf parsley
- Handful of roughly chopped mint
- 1 large or 2 small cucumbers, peeled and thinly sliced
- Lemon, cut into wedges

---

## Preparation

1. Make the chickpeas: Heat oven to 425 degrees. Pour chickpeas into a small baking dish, about 6 inches by 8 inches, they should be crowded together in a thick layer, not spread out. Pour in olive oil just until chickpeas are covered. (This will look like a lot, but don’t worry: It forms part of the dressing.)
2. Add garlic, cumin, paprika, and 2 big pinches each of salt and pepper. Stir gently to combine. Bake until oil is bubbling around the chickpeas and they are turning reddish-brown, 35 to 40 minutes. Set aside to cool.
3. Make tahini sauce: Add tahini to a bowl and whisk in lemon juice and garlic. The tahini will thicken and clump, slowly add warm water 1 tablespoon at a time, whisking until it is creamy and pourable. Taste and season with salt, pepper and more lemon juice, if desired. Set aside.
4. Cook the greens: Place a large frying pan over medium heat, drizzle lightly with olive oil. When hot, add the greens in batches, add a big pinch of salt. Stir until wilted and tender. Set aside.
5. When ready to serve, in a large bowl, gently mix chickpeas and their cooking oil with the cooked greens, half the pita chips, and the chopped herbs. Divide chickpea mixture in 4 shallow bowls. Top with cucumber slices. Drizzle thickly with the tahini sauce and scatter remaining pita chips on top. Place a lemon wedge on the side and serve, passing extra tahini sauce at the table.

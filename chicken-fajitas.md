## Ingredients

- Finely grated zest of 1 lime ( 2 tsps)
- ¼ cup fresh lime juice
- Salt and black pepper
- 2 teaspoons cumin
- 2 tablespoons adobo sauce from canned chipotles in adobo
- 1 chipotle chile in adobo, seeded and minced (optional)
- ¼ cup plus 2 tablespoon olive oil
- 4 large garlic cloves, minced
- 1.5 pounds chicken breast

- 1 red or yellow onion, halved and sliced
- 2 red or orange plus 1 green bell peppers, seeded and sliced ¼ inch thick
- 1 jalapeño or 2 serrano chiles, minced
- ¼ cup chopped cilantro
- 4 large flour or 8 corn tortillas
- 1 tablespoon oil
- 1 romaine heart, sliced crosswise
- Salsa fresca, for serving (see recipe)
- Queso fresco, for sprinkling

---

## Preparation

1. In a small bowl, combine lime zest and juice, 1/2 teaspoon salt, 1 teaspoon ground cumin, adobo sauce and optional minced chipotle, 1/4 cup olive oil and half the garlic. Mix well. Set aside 2 tablespoons of the marinade.
2. Season chicken with salt and pepper and place in a resealable bag. Pour in marinade and seal. Move chicken around to coat well, refrigerate chicken for 30 minutes (or up to 12 hours). Flip the bag over from time to time to redistribute marinade.
3. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat and add 2 tablespoons oil. When oil is hot, add onions and cook, stirring until they soften and begin to color, 3 to 4 minutes. Stir in bell peppers and chile and cook, stirring, until peppers begin to soften, 3 to 4 minutes.
4. Turn heat to medium, add remaining garlic and cumin and salt to taste, and cook, stirring often, until peppers are nicely seared, softened and beginning to caramelize, 5 to 8 minutes.
5. Pour in reserved 2 tablespoons marinade and scrape bottom of pan to deglaze. Add half the cilantro, and stir together. Taste and adjust seasoning. Remove from heat but keep warm.
6. Heat oil over medium-high heat in a large, heavy skillet. Remove chicken breasts from marinade, reserving marinade, and add to pan, rounded side down. Sear for 3 to 4 minutes, until lightly charred. Flip breasts over, pour in marinade, cover pan and reduce heat to medium. Cook 12 to 15 minutes, flipping over from time to time. Transfer to a cutting board and cover with foil. Let sit 5 to 10 minutes, then cut into 1/2- to 3/4-inch thick strips.
7. To serve, arrange chicken and lettuce on a platter. Sprinkle with cilantro. Serve vegetables on the same platter or separately. Serve with warm tortillas, salsa and queso fresco.

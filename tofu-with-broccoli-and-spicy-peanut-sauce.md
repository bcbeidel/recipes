## **INGREDIENTS**

- 1 14 oz. / 400 g block firm (or extra firm tofu)
- 1 Tablespoon soy sauce
- 1 Tablespoon Sriracha
- 4 Tablespoon Neutral Oil
- 2 large bunches broccoli (~ 2 pounds)
- 1/2 small onion, chopped
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 1/2 cup Spicy Peanut Sauce
- 2 teaspoons rice vinegar
- 1 bunch scallions, white and light green parts only, minced (~2/3 cupts)
- 4 lime wedges

---

## Preparation

### Cook The Tofu

1. Set a large skillet over medium-high heat and add soy sauce, sriracha, and 1 tablespoon weather, stirring to combine.
2. Add to the tofu to the pan and using a spatula or small spoon, gently flip the batons a few times to cover them with the glaze. Let sit for 2 minutes and wait for everything to start sizzling a little
3. Using a spatula, gently flip the tofu batons and cook for another two minutes, drizzling in 1 tablespoon of the oil to prevent sticking.
4. When they are deep orange and look lacquered, about 2 minutes more, remove them from the skillet to a wire rack and add remaining 3 Tablespoons oil to the skillet

### Cook The Broccoli

1. Add the broccoli, onion, salt, and pepper to the skillet and stir only occasionally, allowing the broccoli to get crispy and brown in patches, 10-12 minutes. Using a slotted spoon, evenly distribute the broccoli among 4 shallow dinner bowls. Place the tofu batons on top of the broccoli

### Combine And Eat

1. In a small bowl or measuring cup, whisk the peanut sauce with the rice vinegar until it reaches a dressing-like consistency. Drizzle a little on top of each bowel, along with scallions, a squeeze of lime, and sesame seeds, if using. Alternatively, place all the toppings in small bowls on the tables and let diners garnish their bowls themselves.

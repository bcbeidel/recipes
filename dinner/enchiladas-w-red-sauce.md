## Ingredients

**Red Enchilada Sauce**

- 2 Tablespoons neutral oil
- 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
- 1/4 cup chili powder
- 2 cups vegetable stock
- 1 (14 oz) can tomato puree
- 2 teaspoons dried orgenao
- 2 teaspoons smoked paprika
- 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- salt to taste

**Enchiladas**

- 2 tablespoons neutral oil
- 1/2 small yellow onion, chopped
- 1 large garlic clove, minced
- salt and pepper to taste
- 3 cups cooked pinto beans, or 2 15-oz cans rinsed and drained
- 3 tablespoon chopped mild-green hatch chilies
- 1/2 teaspoon cumin
- 7 or 8, 6-in tortillas
- 2 cups shredded chedder
- 3 cups red enchilada sauce
- 2 tablespoons red onion, minced

---

## Preparation

### Sauce Preparation

- In medium saucepan, heat the oil over medium heat
- Add flour, stirring with a wooden spoon, stirring until golden brown, 1 minute.
- Add chili powder until it looks darkened, 30 seconds.
- Whisk the stock, tomato puree, oregano, paprika, cayenne, cumin, salt and stir to combine. Bring to a boil, reduce heat to low, and cook until slightly darkened in color about 15 minutes.
- Use immediately, or refrigerate for up to 5 days, or freeze up to 3 months.

### Enchiladas

- Preheat oven to 375
- In large deep skillet set over medium heat, combine oil, onion, garlic, salt, pepper. Cook until onion softens, bout two minutes
- Add beans, chilies, cumin, and more salt and pepper until warmed through, 3-4 minutes. Mash beans slightly with a fork, add a few tablespoons of water if they seem dry
- Brush a 9x13 pan with oil.
- Spread 2-3 tablespoons of bean spread down the middle of the tortilla. Spread some cheese on top. Roll tortilla around the filling and place it seam side down. Repeat with remaining tortillas, beans and cheese. Reserve half the cheese for topping.
- Smother the tortillas with enchilada sauce. Top with remaining cheese and red onion. Bake until the cheese is bubbly and golden, 15-20 minutes. Serve with desired toppings.

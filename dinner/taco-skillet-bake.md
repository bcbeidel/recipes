## Ingredients

**Vegetables & Seasoning**

- 2 Tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 1 small zucchini, finely diced - (1 1/2 cups)
- 1 small red bell pepper, seeded and finely diced (1 1/2 cups)
- 3/4 cup finely diced yellow onion (1/4 medium)
- 1/2 cup seeded and finely diced poblano pepper (1 small)
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

**Meat And Seasoning**

- 1 pound ground turkey (preferably dark meat)
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- 1 teaspoon chili powder
- 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1/4 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)

**Broth Sauce**

- 3 tablespoon tomato paste
- 3/4 cup low-sodium chicken broth

**Topping**

- 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese (optional)

**For Serving**

- 1 avocado, diced
- 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro leaves
- 2 radishes sliced and cut into matchsticks
- 1/2 jalapeno, thinly sliced (optional)
- 1 large bag, grain free tortilla chips, or plantain chips

---

## Preparation

1. Preheat oven to 375 F.
2. Heat the olive oil in a large cast-iron skillet over medium heat. Add the zucchini, bell pepper, onion, poblano pepper, and garlic. Season with teh salt and pepper and cook, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are tender, about 5 minutes.
3. Add the ground turkey, paprika, chili powder, oregano, cumin, and cayenne pepper (if using). Cook the turkey, breaking it up with the back of a wooden spoon, until it is cooked and no longer pink, about 5 minutes.
4. Reduce the heat to medium and stir in the tomato paste until it is incorporated with the meat mixture. Add 1/2 cup of the broth and continue cooking while stirring until the broth has reduced, about 2 minutes
5. Remove the skillet from heat and add the remaining 1/4 cup broth and stir to combine. Evenly spread the meat mixture across the skillet and sprinkle the top with cheese if using. Transfer the pan to the oven and back until the cheese is hot and bubbly, about 8 minutes.
6. To serve, top with diced avocado, cilantro, radishes, and sliced jalapeño, if using, and dig in with chips

> Description
## Ingredients

- 1 pound ground chicken    
- 1/3 cup panko breadcrumbs, plus more for rolling the meatballs 
- 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese, plus more for garnish
- 1 tablespoon Italian seasoning (or 1 teaspoon each dried thyme, dried oregano, and dried parsley) 
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, finely chopped, plus more for garnish
- 2 garlic cloves, grated, or 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 egg
- Cooking spray
- 1 (24-ounce) jar marinara sauce (I love Rao’s Roasted Garlic Sauce)
- 4 ounces any mozzarella (ciliegine, shredded, burrata — you pick!)
- Something carby for serving — either a good loaf of French bread or sourdough, 1 pound of cooked noodles (any type or shape!), or cooked cauliflower rice

---
## Preparation

1. Preheat oven to 425°F.

2. Place 1 pound ground chicken, 1/3 cup panko breadcrumbs, 1/2 cup grated Parmesan, 1 tablespoon Italian seasoning, 1 teaspoon kosher salt, 1/4 cup finely chopped basil, 2 minced garlic cloves, and 1 egg in a large bowl. Beat the egg with a fork, then use your hands to combine all of the ingredients. 

3. Spray the inside of a 10-inch oven-proof skillet all over with cooking spray and set aside.

4. Pour about 1/3 cup panko breadcrumbs into a shallow bowl or plate. Form 10 meatballs out of the chicken mixture, roll each one through the panko to coat them in breadcrumbs (add more breadcrumbs to the plate if needed to coat them all!), and place them in the oiled skillet. Spray with cooking spray, or drizzle with oil.

5. Bake the meatballs for 15 minutes. Remove the skillet from the oven and pour 1 (24-ounce) jar marinara sauce into the skillet around the meatballs so they’re nestled in the sauce. Be careful as you pour! The sauce could splatter a bit since the skillet will be hot.

6. Scatter 4 ounces of mozzarella over top of it all then return the skillet to the oven for another 10 minutes, until the cheese is melty.

7. Scatter more fresh basil and Parmesan over top and serve immediately, straight out of the skillet!

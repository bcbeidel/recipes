## Ingredients

- 1 large bunch broccolini or kale, thick stems separated from the leaves
- ¼ cup olive oil, plus more for drizzling
- 4 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 1 medium red or yellow onion, thinly sliced
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- 2 to 3 tablespoons tomato paste
- Red-pepper flakes (optional)
- 3 (15-ounce) cans white beans, such as cannellini, butter or great Northern, drained and rinsed
- 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
- 1 lemon, halved, for squeezing
- 2 ounces feta
- ½ cup parsley

---

## Preparation

1. Tear broccolini or kale leaves into bite-size pieces and set aside. Chop the stems into about 1/4-inch pieces; set aside.
2. Heat 1/4 cup olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add garlic and onion, and season with salt and pepper. Cook, stirring occasionally, until lightly browned and sizzled at the edges, 4 to 6 minutes.
3. Add tomato paste and a pinch of red-pepper flakes, and stir to coat in the oil. Cook until a nice brick red color, the sugars start to caramelize and the oil turns a nice vibrant fiery orange color, about 2 minutes.
4. Add beans, and season with salt and pepper. Using a wooden spoon or spatula, crush a few beans to release their creamy interior.
5. Add the broth and reserved stems, and season with salt and pepper. Bring to a simmer and cook until you’ve reached your desired consistency (less time for a brothier soup, more time for a thicker stew), 15 to 20 minutes.
6. Add broccolini or kale leaves and lemon juice, and stir to wilt the greens. Season with salt, pepper and more red-pepper flakes if you want it spicier.
7. Serve with feta and parsley, and with soft boiled eggs, if you like.

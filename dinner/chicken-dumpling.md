## Ingredients

### **Dumpling Wrapper**

- 200g all-purpose flour (flour with at least 10% protein)
- 105g room temp water
- 1⁄4 tsp salt

### **Chicken Filling**

- 1 pound chicken thighs
- 1⁄4 cup minced celery
- 1⁄4 cup minced napa cabbage
- 1⁄2 cup green onion, minced
- 1 tsp. salt
- 2 Tbsp. ginger and garlic paste*
- 1⁄2 cup cilantro, minced
- 1 egg
- 1 Tbsp. sesame oil
- 1⁄2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
- 1 Tbsp. soy sauce

### **Dipping Sauce**

equal parts soy sauce and rice vinegar, with a pinch of sugar, squeeze of lime juice and red pepper flakes

## Preparation

### Dumpling Wrappers

1. In a large mixing bowl, combine flour (if using starch, sift it together) and salt if  
    using. Using chopsticks or a fork, mix in half the water, then the last half until  
    the mix is shaggy.  
    
2. Begin to use hands to forms a dough.
3. When dough came together and they’re are no dry bits left in mixing bowl, take out dough from mixing bowl to a work surface if you prefer and start kneading the dough. It will be soft but not sticky. Knead for 5 to 6 minutes until smooth.
4. Cover with plastic and rest 20 minutes.
5. Knead again for 5 minutes until the dough springs back when pressed with your fingertip. Cover with a damp towel and rest for 1 hour.
6. Divide dough to half and shape each pieces as 6” to 7” long log. Cut each logs halves and then cut into 1⁄2” wide pieces (they shuld weigh between 12-14g). Repeat to create about 25 wrappers.
7. Working on one piece of dough at a time and meanwhile cover rest of them with a damp towel to prevent from drying. Bring one dough at a time to work surface, slightly flatten it with your palm.
8. Using a rolling pin, flatten dough into 31⁄2” circle. Place dumpling wrapper on a flour dusted plate and dust each dumpling wrappers so they won’t stick to each others. Use the back and forth method in the video to create a circle that is thicker on the bottom and thinner around the edges. Stack wrappers as you finish and dust flour in-between them.

### Chicken Filling

1. Cube and partially freeze the chicken thighs.
2. Combine the minced vegetables with 1⁄2 tsp of salt and allow to sit 10-20 minutes to remove excess water.
3. Add the chicken thighs to the food processor and pulse a number of times to create a minced meat. Transfer to a bowl.
4. Squeeze out the liquid from the vegetables and add to the chicken. Stir in the remaining salt and other ingredients.
5. Put one wrapper at a time one a clean working surface and place a scant teaspoon of filling in the middle. Using your fingers, crimp and fold into a traditional half-moon shape and push the edges together tightly.
6. Once formed, gently push the top of the dumpling down to make it “sit”. Place on a pan lightly dusted with arrowroot. Repeat until finished. Cook immediately or freeze for later.

### Cooking The Dumplings

1. In a separate pot place 1 inch of water and bring to boil. Place a colander over top, with a lid.
2. Over medium heat, add 2 tablespoons of oil to the pan
3. Pan fry the dumplings for 2-3 minutes, allow for them to brown.
4. Place fried dumplings into colander, cover with lid. Allow to steam for 4-5 minutes. Check that they are cooked through.
